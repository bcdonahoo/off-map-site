The job of a sales development representative used to be research, write, send, repeat.

That job is over — at least the way it was done.

## What changed

Three things converged in the last eighteen months:

1. **Intent data got cheap.** Bombora, G2, Clearbit, LinkedIn Sales Navigator — the signals are everywhere. What was missing was a system to act on them faster than your competitors.
2. **LLMs got good at structured output.** Not marketing copy. Structured, account-specific context: what this company does, what they recently launched, why that matters to your offer. Claude can produce that in under a second per account.
3. **Enrichment APIs got composable.** Clay didn't invent waterfall enrichment, but they made it accessible. Seventy-five data sources, one interface, API-first.

Put those three together and you get something new: a pipeline that generates qualified, personalized outreach at the speed of a script.

## What GTM Engineering actually means

It is not marketing automation. It is not "AI-generated spam."

GTM Engineering is the discipline of building systems that:
- Identify in-market accounts from real signals
- Enrich those accounts with contact and context data
- Generate account-specific research context with AI
- Route that context through a human review gate
- Launch personalized multi-step sequences via dedicated infrastructure
- Attribute results from signal to meeting to close

The human is still in the loop. The machine just removes the parts that should never have required a human.

## The moat is the system

The companies that figure this out in the next twelve months will have a structural cost advantage over those that don't.

Not because they are sending more emails. Because they are sending *better* ones, faster, cheaper, with less headcount — and they know exactly what's working.

That is the moat. And it compounds.
